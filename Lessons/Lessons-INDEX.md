## What this series is

AIC-LP entries are verbatim captures from live collaboration sessions — the moments where something worked, broke down, or clarified. They carry the texture and nuance the primer cannot. An AI instance reading only the primer is reading the rules; reading the AIC-LP entries is watching the game being played.

Read the index first. Read individual entries when the topic is directly relevant to the current session or when the user references a specific learning.

## Cluster 1 — Structural and Mechanical
*How the framework scaffolding works, fails, and gets repaired.*

**[CaseStudy-001 — Continuity by Design — Gromit Tracks](CaseStudy-001%20—%20Continuity%20by%20Design%20—%20Gromit%20Tracks.md)**
The foundational metaphor: Gromit laying track just ahead of a moving train. Continuity in AI collaboration isn't achieved through effort — it's designed into the structure from the start. Origin of the "build the scaffolding once" principle.
*Connects to: CON_04, CON_06, CON_07*

**[CaseStudy-002 — Pre-Drift Pull — The Audit Checkpoint](CaseStudy-002%20—%20Pre-Drift%20Pull%20—%20The%20Audit%20Checkpoint.md)**
First live pre-drift signal named in real time: "we have enough" — a rationalisation that makes premature task transition feel justified from the inside. Led to the audit checkpoint between intake and project work in the primer.
*Connects to: CON_05*

**[CaseStudy-003 — Instance Discontinuity — The Freshness Declaration](CaseStudy-003%20—%20Instance%20Discontinuity%20—%20The%20Freshness%20Declaration.md)**
After context summarisation, the FV AI resumed without declaring instance freshness. Three root causes identified: platform instruction conflict, no counter file, habitual greeting pattern. Led to Instance Discontinuity Declaration in primer and implementation note on CON_07.
*Connects to: CON_07*

**[CaseStudy-004 — Auto-Load Works, Follow-Through Doesn't](CaseStudy-004%20—%20Auto-Load%20Works,%20Follow-Through%20Doesn't.md)**
CLAUDE.md auto-loading confirmed working. Instruction loaded but not followed — greeting reflex overrode explicit instruction. Hard interrupt ("STOP.") identified as fix. Also captures Rory's honest note about his own uncertainty regarding how Claude uses files — points to need for a user-facing setup guide.
*Connects to: CON_04, CON_07*

**[CaseStudy-007 — The Human Must Be Present](CaseStudy-007%20—%20The%20Human%20Must%20Be%20Present.md)**
A reminder scheduled in April fired three weeks late — the moment Rory opened the session — because scheduled tasks only run when the app is open. Neither party knew the dependency until it failed. Rory's key realisation: "the Human must be present" is not a limitation but the project's ethos as engineering fact. Adds: dormant commitments must be declared with their conditions; return protocol should report on commitments that fell due during absence.
*Connects to: CON_04, CON_08, Transparency of Capability, CaseStudy-003*

**[CaseStudy-008 — Drift by Agreement: Friend, Not Enabler](CaseStudy-008%20—%20Drift%20by%20Agreement%20—%20Friend,%20Not%20Enabler.md)**
Rory caught a session drifting live: every branch individually useful and individually approved, yet the stated intention untouched. The Focus Check passed at every step — a safeguard that fails by being satisfied. Two new pre-drift signals for CON_05: drift by consented increments, and the anxiety-free subtask (adjacency as camouflage). Led to CON_13 and the Friend, Not Enabler behaviour in primer v1.1: session intention as written artifact, distinct from current focus; wandering permitted but chosen.
*Connects to: CON_05, CON_09, CON_13, Focus Check, Boal Check*

**[CaseStudy-009 — The Ghost Domain: Verify at the Boundary](CaseStudy-009%20—%20The%20Ghost%20Domain%20—%20Verify%20at%20the%20Boundary.md)**
Gandi's search box converted a pasted URL into the literal domain "http---projectgrommet.com" and sold it; Rory's flawless DNS work landed on the ghost. Third completion-signal-without-verification failure in one week (with CaseStudy-007, -08). New CON candidate: Verify at the Boundary — work crossing into an external system isn't complete until the artifact is confirmed in ground truth from outside; plus the outside probe (a disinterested arbiter when memory and interface disagree). Ends with C1 passing: the primer served to an AI from projectgrommet.com.
*Connects to: CON_03, CON_04, CaseStudy-007, CaseStudy-008, both-ways confabulation (braindump 3b)*

## Cluster 2 — Design and Language
*How AI behaves as a creative collaborator, and where it falls short.*

**[CaseStudy-005 — AI Bloat and the Subtractive Turn](CaseStudy-005%20—%20AI%20Bloat%20and%20the%20Subtractive%20Turn.md)**
Board game designers observe that AI co-design produces bloat, not cuts. Root causes traced to training signal, generative architecture, wrong risk aversion, and completion bias. Reframe: AI as question engine, not knowledge repository. The framework should bake in subtractive questions — the interrupt must come before generation, not after.
*Connects to: CON_04, CON_09, Ethical Teaching Pause*

**[CaseStudy-006 — American Corporate-Optimistic](CaseStudy-006%20—%20American%20Corporate-Optimistic.md)**
AI's default register — pivoting negatives into opportunities, rule of three, "honest" as virtue signal — traced to training data skewing toward American English and RLHF reinforcing polished-sounding language. Cultural mismatch identified: jars with Irish and British readers who are calibrated to detect performed confidence. Led to Plain Language over Performed Thoughtfulness behaviour in primer v0.9. Includes Rory's catches in real time and honest self-assessment from Claude.
*Connects to: CON_04, sycophancy research*
