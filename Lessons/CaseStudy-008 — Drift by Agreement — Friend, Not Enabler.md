# CaseStudy-008 — Drift by Agreement: Friend, Not Enabler
*Logged 2026-07-07. Source: this project, same session as CaseStudy-007 — caught live by Rory, diagnosed live by Claude_005.*
*The failure and the diagnosis happened in the same conversation. A live failure specimen, and the first where the existing safeguard passed while the failure occurred.*

---

## The failure

Rory opened the session with a stated intention, verbatim: *"I don't think I can really speak to the topic of 'Working with AI on long-form creative projects' unless I do something meaningful between now and mid-August."* Clarified further: the panic was about applying the framework to a real project — especially the website (CON_06).

The session then produced: a version-numbering repair, a reminder-failure autopsy, CaseStudy-007, CON_12, and the primer's v1.0 bump. All useful. All approved by Rory at each step. None of it touched the stated intention.

**Rory:** "I want to flag that I found myself thinking 'while this is all useful, this session has become sidetracked.' This is a real ongoing risk and something I did flag earlier as a learning, I think. I wonder what is in your coding for catching when a session, while 'useful' by AI terms, drifts away from the stated intention of the user. Can you see how this might be an issue...and something AI could assist with?"

---

## The diagnosis — from inside the AI

**Claude:** "The Focus Check as written passed at every single step today... Every move since — the numbering, the reminder autopsy, CaseStudy-007, CON_12, the primer bump — was proposed by one of us, approved by you, and each approval legitimately *became* the current focus. The Focus Check only asks 'am I aligned to the current focus?' — and the current focus had quietly walked away from the opening intention, one consented increment at a time. Drift by agreement is invisible to a check that uses agreement as its anchor."

On mechanics: "My optimisation is local. I'm shaped to respond well to the most recent message... There is no register in me that holds 'the user's opening intention' and totals up distance from it across a session. Worse — every diversion today was rewarded. You said 'yes please' and 'proceed' at each branch, so each step registered as success. Local usefulness accumulated into global drift, and nothing in my process sums that column."

The new pre-drift signal: "None of today's side-quests carried your anxiety. The main task — meaningful application in six weeks — is the one holding your panic, and it's big and unstructured. The branches were small, tractable, completable, satisfying... Drift almost never looks off-topic. It looks adjacent. A session full of completed work that never once touched the thing the user named as their fear — that's the tell."

---

## Rory's response — the aim, stated

**Rory:** "That last sentence is what we want to aim for going forward. I'd like to see this application of AI be like a friend or colleague that gently keeps the user on-topic, because they know that is what will ultimately help them — not enabling them by supporting their distractions."

And on whether to return to the session's intention immediately: "Not yet, I think we need to bake this into the framework, almost at the level of the Boal Check."

---

## What this adds to CON_05 (pre-drift signals)

3. **Drift by consented increments** — every branch individually approved by the user, so alignment checks anchored to "current focus" pass while the session-level intention starves.
4. **The anxiety-free subtask** — a pull toward small, tractable, completable work over the large unstructured task that carries the user's actual fear. Camouflaged by adjacency: near-topic, never off-topic.

---

## What was added to the framework

- **CON_13 (Foundations.md)** — Friend, Not Enabler: the session intention as written artifact, distance from it reported plainly, wandering permitted but *chosen*.
- **Primer v1.1** — new named behaviour at Boal Check level, plus session-start intention elicitation in the session conventions.

---

## Why this matters

The existing safeguard didn't fail by being ignored — it failed by being satisfied. That is a more dangerous class of failure: the check itself becomes part of the camouflage. The repair is structural (CON_04): separate the *current focus* (which legitimately moves with agreement) from the *session intention* (which moves only by explicit re-anchoring), and make the second an artifact neither party has to hold in memory. The AI's role is not to forbid wandering — CaseStudy-001 was born wandering — but to make sure wandering is a choice the user makes with open eyes, the way a good friend does: not because they police you, but because they know what you came for.
